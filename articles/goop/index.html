We Tried Goop's Wellness Protocols for 30 Days. Here's What Actually Happened

By Maren Ellis · 7 min read · Wellness & Body

I want to be upfront: I went into this skeptical. Goop — with its prices, its occasional pseudoscience, and its very particular aesthetic — has always occupied a complicated place in my mind. I think it is simultaneously worth taking seriously and worth questioning rigorously, which is exactly the spirit in which I tried their current lineup of protocols for thirty days.

What I Actually Tried

The current Goop wellness framework centers on a few pillars: their supplement line, a morning movement practice, an emphasis on organic whole foods and seed cycling for hormonal support, and their skin protocol. I committed to all of it for thirty days, bought what I needed to buy, and kept notes.

The Things That Genuinely Worked

The magnesium supplement

I was sleeping better by week two. Genuinely, noticeably better. Magnesium glycinate is not a Goop invention — it's widely available and well-researched for sleep support — but I had never been consistent enough with it to feel the effects. Their formula is good. You could also buy magnesium glycinate anywhere for a fraction of the price.

The seed cycling practice

This one I had written off entirely. The data here is modest, but by week three my premenstrual symptoms were noticeably milder than usual. I kept doing it.

The movement protocol

Thirty minutes, five days a week, of intentional movement combining flexibility and strength work. By the end of thirty days I felt more at home in my body than I had in years. No caveats here.

The Things That Were Mostly Marketing

The $98 vitamin C serum performed identically to a $28 alternative. The detox powder made me feel no different than drinking a green smoothie I made myself. And the idea that you need a specialized protocol for hydration — just drink water — is the kind of thing that should make you suspicious of any wellness brand.

"The most valuable thing Goop sells isn't any product. It's the permission to take your own health seriously. That part, you can have for free."

Thirty days in, I feel better. The lesson: read everything Goop puts out, take the ideas seriously, replicate what you can with less expensive alternatives, and spend the money you save on a really good reformer Pilates class.
