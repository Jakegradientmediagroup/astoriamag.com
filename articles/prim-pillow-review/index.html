The $68 Pillow That Might Be the Most Underrated Marriage Investment We've Ever Made

By Priya Holloway · 7 min read · Relationships & Marriage

I want to tell you about a pillow, and I want to be upfront that this is going to sound absurd before it sounds true. My husband and I have spent real money over eleven years of marriage trying to keep intimacy alive — date nights, a marriage retreat, a few sessions with a counselor during a hard stretch a few years back. All of it mattered. None of it made quite as immediate a difference as the sixty-eight dollar pillow currently sitting on our bed.

The pillow is called the Prim, made by a company called Tabu, and it's specifically designed to support couples during sex — something that sounds clinical until you actually understand why it matters, and considerably more once you've tried it yourselves.

Why a Pillow, Specifically

Here's the thing nobody tells you in your twenties, when your body cooperates with whatever position you land in without complaint: bodies change. After two kids, a herniated disc I'm still rehabbing, and eleven years of a mattress slowly losing its support, ordinary intimacy started coming with a quiet tax neither of us wanted to admit to each other — a sore lower back the next morning, an awkward angle that required more effort than either of us wanted to bring up out loud.

Pelvic floor physical therapists and sex educators have been recommending wedge-style pillows for exactly this reason for years, specifically because the right elevation changes the angle of the hips in a way that takes real pressure off the lower back and knees, while also making certain positions considerably more comfortable and satisfying for both partners. The catch has always been that the wedge pillows built for this were clinical-looking, firm, and the kind of thing you'd never leave out on an actual bed. The Prim was designed to solve that specific problem — something that functions like a physical therapy tool but looks and feels like an ordinary throw pillow you'd be glad to have on your bed at all.

"A physician who specializes in sexual health has publicly praised Tabu for building products that treat intimacy as something worth genuine engineering, not an afterthought."

What Actually Changed for Us

I'll be specific, because vague enthusiasm isn't useful to anyone actually considering whether to spend the money. The elevation the Prim provides changes the angle enough that positions we used to modify or avoid because of my back became not just possible again, but genuinely more comfortable than they'd been in years, even before the disc issue. My husband, who carries tension in his wrists from a desk job, noticed almost immediately that positions requiring him to support his weight on his hands and arms were considerably easier with the added support underneath.

None of this is dramatic in the way marriage advice usually promises dramatic transformation. It's a quieter thing: intimacy that used to come with a small, unspoken physical cost now simply doesn't, which means neither of us is subtly avoiding it the way tired, sore bodies quietly avoid things without ever saying so directly.

The Positions Worth Knowing About

For anyone considering this, here's where we've found the most actual difference:

Missionary

The added elevation allows for a more controlled angle and considerably easier access for both partners, without either person needing to hold an awkward position to make things work.

Her on top

Lifting his hips slightly gives her more control with noticeably less pressure on her knees, which matters more than you'd expect once you've been married more than a decade.

Doggy Style

The angle takes real pressure off both partners' wrists and hands, which was the single biggest difference for my husband specifically.

Spooning

A little extra support between you accommodates however your two bodies actually fit together, rather than forcing an adjustment that works for neither of you.

A Word on Why This Matters More Than It Sounds Like It Should

Physical intimacy in a long marriage is not a minor detail sitting off to the side of the "real" work of staying connected. It's one of the more reliable barometers of a marriage's overall health, and a quiet decline in comfort or ease in that specific area tends to bleed into everything else — patience, warmth, the sense of being truly chosen by your spouse day after day. Something as unglamorous as a pillow addressing a real, physical barrier to that intimacy isn't a small thing dressed up as a big one. It's a genuinely practical fix for something that touches far more of a marriage than people expect a pillow to.

A Closing Word

We didn't need a retreat or a breakthrough conversation to fix this particular piece of our marriage. We needed better support, quite literally, for bodies that have changed over eleven years together. If you and your husband have quietly been working around the same kind of physical friction we were, before you invest in anything bigger, this might be the smaller, more practical place to start.
